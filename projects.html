Argon Molecular Dynamics Simulation

Computational Physics · Molecular Dynamics · Scientific Computing

Independent Project

This project implements a two-dimensional molecular dynamics simulation of argon atoms using the Lennard–Jones potential and Newton’s laws of motion. The equations are integrated using the velocity Verlet method, and the simulation is visualized directly inside a Jupyter notebook.

The system is evolved in reduced units with periodic boundary conditions, and key thermodynamic quantities such as energy, temperature, and pressure are computed over time. The simulation also includes animated particle motion and comparison of velocity distributions with the Maxwell distribution.

  • Lennard–Jones interactions with periodic boundary conditions
  • Velocity Verlet integration scheme
  • Energy, temperature, and pressure analysis
  • Real-time particle animation
