Understanding the Structural Organization of Biomolecular Assemblies Using Graph-Theoretic Techniques

Computational Biophysics

Developed a Python-based computational pipeline to construct and analyse graph representations of biomolecular assemblies derived from structural (PDB) data. Implemented distance-based interaction modelling, adjacency construction, and network-theoretic analysis to investigate structural organisation in biomolecular complexes.

Supervised by Dr. Debayan Chakraborty
Department of Theoretical Physics
The Institute of Mathematical Sciences (IMSc), Chennai

Full implementation maintained in a private GitHub research repository.
Public overview: biomolecular-graph-analysis

Anti-cancer Drug Design: Targeting ABL1

Cheminformatics · Computational Drug Discovery

Designed and implemented a ligand-based virtual screening workflow to identify candidate inhibitors of the ABL1 tyrosine kinase. The pipeline integrates physicochemical filtering, PAINS removal, molecular fingerprinting (MACCS and Morgan), similarity screening against a reference inhibitor (Dasatinib), enrichment evaluation, Butina clustering, maximum common substructure analysis, and a supervised neural network classifier to prioritise active compounds.

Independent research project
Awarded 2nd Position for paper presentation at the International Conference “Advances in Pharmaceutical Sciences: Bridging Academia and Industry”, St. Stephen’s College.

Public repository: abl1-virtual-screening-ml
