About

I am drawn to questions about how biomolecules are organised and interact with one another, and to computational methods that make such systems easier to understand without losing physical and chemical meaning.

Working with biomolecular data has often involved translating physical systems into graph-based representations, where relationships between components can be studied systematically. This has led me to develop Python-based analysis pipelines to process biomolecular structural data, construct graph-based representations, and explore patterns that are not immediately visible from raw molecular coordinates.

I have also been engaging with molecular dynamics simulations as a way to connect structural organisation with underlying motion and interactions at the atomic scale. Through this work, questions of representation, modelling assumptions, and interpretability have become central to how I analyse results and draw conclusions.

I am broadly interested in computational biophysics, soft matter and molecular modelling, particularly in areas where ideas from chemistry, physics, and mathematics intersect. This page is a brief reflection of what I am currently exploring and the way I like to think about scientific work.
